## Kalkulator (/kalkulator) — 6 Q&A

Q: What are AGA zones in Norway?
A: AGA (arbeidsgiveravgift) is the employer's social security contribution in Norway. Norway is divided into 7 geographical zones with rates ranging from 0% in the northernmost regions (Zone 5a) to 14.1% in major urban areas (Zone 1). The zone is determined by the municipality where the business is registered, not where the employee lives.

Q: How is holiday pay (feriepenger) calculated in Norway?
A: Holiday pay in Norway is calculated as a percentage of the previous year's gross earnings. The standard rate is 10.2% (for 4 weeks + 1 day of vacation). Employees covered by tariff agreements with 5 weeks of vacation receive 12%. Employees over 60 years old get an additional 2.3 percentage points. Holiday pay replaces regular salary during the vacation period.

Q: What is OTP (obligatorisk tjenestepensjon)?
A: OTP is Norway's mandatory occupational pension scheme. Employers must contribute a minimum of 2% of employee salary between 1G and 12G to a pension fund. Many employers contribute more, up to a maximum of 7% of salary. The contributions are invested and grow tax-deferred until retirement.

Q: What do NPV and IRR mean in investment analysis?
A: NPV (Net Present Value) calculates the current value of future cash flows minus the initial investment, using a chosen discount rate. A positive NPV means the investment is expected to be profitable. IRR (Internal Rate of Return) is the discount rate that makes NPV equal to zero. Both are standard methods for evaluating whether an investment project is worth pursuing.

Q: How does the salary vs. dividend comparison work?
A: The calculator compares the total cost to the employer and the net amount received by the owner when taking money out of a company as salary versus dividends. Salary incurs AGA, holiday pay, and OTP costs but is tax-deductible for the company. Dividends are taxed at 22% corporate tax first, then the owner pays personal tax on dividends (using the shielding deduction method). The optimal split depends on income level and AGA zone.

Q: What depreciation rates apply to Norwegian business assets?
A: Norwegian tax depreciation uses the declining balance method across 10 asset groups (a through j). Rates range from 2% for office buildings (group i) to 30% for trucks and buses (group c). Common rates include 20% for machinery and equipment (group d), 10% for vehicles, and 4% for commercial buildings (group h).

## Boliglan (/boliglan) — 5 Q&A

Q: What is the difference between annuity and serial loans in Norway?
A: With an annuity loan (annuitetslaan), you pay the same total amount each month throughout the loan term. With a serial loan (serielaan), you repay a fixed amount of principal each month plus decreasing interest. Serial loans have lower total interest costs, but annuity loans offer more predictable budgeting.

Q: What is the mortgage stress test in Norway?
A: Under Utlaansforskriften, Norwegian banks must verify that borrowers can afford their mortgage if interest rates rise by 3 percentage points, with a minimum test rate of 7%. This stress test ensures borrowers are not over-leveraged.

Q: How much equity do I need to buy a home in Norway?
A: You need at least 15% equity (egenkapital) of the property's market value for a primary residence. For secondary homes in Oslo, the requirement is 40%. Young buyers (under 34) can use BSU savings to help meet the equity requirement.

Q: What is dokumentavgift?
A: Dokumentavgift is a 2.5% property transfer tax paid on the market value when you register ownership of a property (tinglysing). It applies to self-owned homes but not to housing cooperative units (borettslag). In addition, you pay a tinglysingsgebyr of 585 kr per document.

Q: Can I deduct mortgage interest on my taxes?
A: Yes, in Norway you get a 22% tax deduction on all interest payments, including mortgage interest. This means for every 10,000 kr you pay in interest, you save 2,200 kr in taxes.

## Personlig (/personlig) — 4 Q&A

Q: How do I create a personal budget?
A: Start by listing all income sources. Then categorize expenses: fixed costs (rent, insurance, loans), variable necessities (food, transport, utilities), and discretionary spending. Subtract total expenses from income to find your monthly surplus or deficit.

Q: How much does it really cost to own a car in Norway?
A: Total car ownership costs include depreciation, fuel or electricity, insurance, annual road tax, maintenance, tolls, and parking. For a typical car, total costs range from 4,000-8,000 kr per month depending on the car's value, age, and how much you drive.

Q: How is net salary calculated in Norway?
A: Your gross salary is reduced by income tax (trinnskatt at progressive rates), social security contribution (trygdeavgift at 7.9%), and standard deductions. If you earn below the frikort threshold (70,000 kr in 2026), no tax is withheld.

Q: What is frikort and who qualifies?
A: Frikort means you pay no tax on income up to 70,000 kr per year (2026). It is primarily used by students and part-time workers. Once you exceed it, normal tax rates apply above the threshold.

## Avgift (/avgift) — 3 Q&A

Q: What are the VAT rates in Norway?
A: Norway has three VAT rates: 25% standard, 15% for food, and 12% for transport/hotel/cinema. Some services are VAT-exempt (healthcare, education, financial services).

Q: What are MVA adjustment rules (justeringsregler)?
A: If you claimed input VAT on a capital asset and later change its use, you must adjust the VAT previously claimed. The adjustment period is 10 years for real estate and 5 years for other capital assets.

Q: When must I register for VAT in Norway?
A: You must register when taxable turnover exceeds 50,000 kr within a 12-month period. Registration is done through Altinn.

## Selskap (/selskap) — 4 Q&A

Q: What is the difference between AS and ENK in Norway?
A: AS is a limited company with minimum 30,000 kr share capital and limited liability. ENK is a sole proprietorship with no minimum capital but unlimited personal liability.

Q: How much does it cost to register a company in Norway?
A: AS/ASA: 5,570 kr electronically. ANS/DA/KS: 3,070 kr. ENK in Foretaksregisteret: 2,832 kr. NUF: 2,832 kr. All via altinn.no.

Q: What is required to establish an AS in Norway?
A: Minimum 30,000 kr share capital, articles of association, founding document signed by founders, registration through Altinn, and a board of directors. Process takes 1-3 weeks.

Q: How are different company types taxed in Norway?
A: AS pays 22% corporate tax. Dividends taxed at effective ~37.84%. ENK profits taxed as personal income (up to ~49.6% marginal). For owners taking 600,000+ kr, AS with dividend is often more tax-efficient.
